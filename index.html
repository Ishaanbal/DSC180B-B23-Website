Pralsetinib Polypharmacology & Toxicity Knowledge Graph

Team Members: Ishaan Bal, Suchit Bhayani, Ryan Cao, Taranvir Chima

View Full Project on GitHub

Abstract

We explore the unknown off-target space of Pralsetinib, a recently approved RET kinase inhibitor, to predict potential adverse effects. By constructing a neuro-symbolic knowledge graph enriched with biomedical ontologies, we aim to link drug mechanisms directly to toxicity. Using Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) on this graph, we predict off-target proteins and associated diseases/adverse events. Our GNN model successfully recovered all held-out known targets within the top 20 predictions, providing a strong foundation for generating safety-relevant hypotheses for new drugs.

Introduction

Pralsetinib is a recently approved targeted therapy with limited long-term real-world safety data. Post-marketing pharmacovigilance has already revealed unexpected adverse effects beyond its primary on-target mechanisms (e.g., rhabdomyolysis, severe infections). Due to its complex kinase biology, there is a significant "unknown off-target space". In this study, we build a neuro-symbolic knowledge graph that links mechanisms to toxicity, enriching it with Gene Ontology (GO) and target-outcome links. By running a GNN over the full graph, we generate ranked predictions for potential off-target proteins and their associated adverse effects, offering valuable hypotheses for drug safety.

Methods

Knowledge Graph Construction

We extracted edges and nodes from multiple sources, including PubChem (bioactivity, consolidated targets, clinical trials, chemical/gene co-occurrences) and literature text mining for adverse events, forming our initial relationships.

Enrichment & Expansion

We expanded the graph by integrating GO pathways and target-outcome associations. We further expanded the protein set using the STRING PPI network and UniProt human kinases, drastically increasing the candidate space for prediction.

GNN Off-Target Prediction

We trained a Graph Neural Network on the full graph to predict missing (drug, inhibits, protein) and (protein, associated_with, Disease/AE) links. A simple baseline model summing KG edge weights was used for comparison. Path-based reasoning was generated to explain the predicted links.

Results

Knowledge Graph Statistics

The final enriched and expanded graph consists of 1,084 nodes and 1,464 edges. The expansion increased our candidate protein set by approximately 34-fold, resulting in 516 candidates for ranking alongside 13 known Pralsetinib targets.

Prediction Performance

We evaluated our GNN on a held-out test set of known Pralsetinib targets. The model achieved a Recall@5 of 25%, Recall@10 of 50%, and Recall@20 of 100%, successfully recovering all four held-out targets within the top 20 predictions (Mean Rank: 11.5, MRR: 0.147). The multi-hop structure utilized by the GNN significantly outperformed the baseline model.

Interactive Visualization

We developed an interactive PyVis HTML visualization of the knowledge graph to explore the mechanistic pathways linking Pralsetinib to predicted diseases and adverse events.

Conclusion

Our neuro-symbolic, ontology-based approach demonstrates that GNNs can effectively leverage complex biomedical knowledge graphs to predict drug off-targets. By successfully identifying known targets and proposing novel candidates with path-based reasoning, this framework provides a powerful tool for early pharmacovigilance and hypothesis generation regarding the safety profiles of newly approved therapeutics.
